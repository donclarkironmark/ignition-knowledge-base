## 1. Platform Narrative

### What Is Ignition?

Ignition is Ironmark's AI-powered distributed marketing platform. It connects brand strategy to local execution for multilocation brands -- franchise networks, dealer groups, healthcare systems, and distributed retail operations. The platform gives brand leaders unified reporting, AI-driven insights, and campaign tools across every location in their network.

The core problem Ignition solves is structural: multilocation brands spend heavily on marketing but cannot connect that spend to revenue. Print, digital, and local execution are fragmented across vendors. Data lives in silos. Nobody at the brand level can answer "is our marketing working?" at the individual location level, and nobody at the local level knows what to do next.

Ignition bridges the through-channel gap. It gives brands visibility and control -- real-time ROI dashboards, location benchmarking, AI-powered anomaly detection. It gives local operators (franchisees, dealers, clinic managers, branch advisors) the tools, creative, and automation to execute marketing without becoming marketers -- with AI doing the heavy lifting. Every portal, every report, every AI conversation lives under the Ignition brand.

### The Transformation Story

Ironmark has operated as a marketing services provider for over 30 years, serving enterprise brands with print production, direct mail, creative services, and campaign execution. The company already sits inside the marketing operations of multilocation brands -- producing their materials, managing their campaigns, handling their data. Ignition adds an intelligence layer on top of these existing relationships. It converts project-based revenue into recurring platform revenue and transforms Ironmark from a marketing vendor into a platform company. The same AI architecture built for internal product management maps directly to customer-facing marketing intelligence.

### The Through-Channel Model

Most marketing platforms serve only one level -- either brand headquarters (analytics dashboards) or local operators (template libraries). Ignition serves the entire channel, from brand HQ strategy and budget down through regional managers to local operators and their end customers. Brand HQ gets network-level dashboards, compliance tools, and AI-powered insights. Regional managers get benchmarks and alert-based monitoring. Local operators get pre-built campaign kits, AI-generated creative, and fund management. This full-channel coverage is the central differentiator.

## 2. Capability Theme Summaries

Ignition is organized into 7 capability themes, delivered in three compounding phases: Intelligence (visibility), Activation (turning insight into action), and Automation (embedding into daily workflows).

---

### Theme 1: ROI-Based Reporting

**One-line:** Unified dashboard connecting marketing spend to revenue outcomes across all channels, at every location.

**Status:** Production (Live)

**Key Benefits:**
- Automated data ingestion from Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn -- no manual exports or spreadsheets
- Closed-loop attribution connecting upstream ad spend to downstream CRM/POS revenue (not just clicks or impressions)
- Multi-tenant architecture: network, region, and individual location views in one dashboard
- Daily campaign performance metrics per platform with 7 timeframe options

**Target User Persona:** Brand CMOs, VPs of Marketing, regional marketing managers, franchise operators who need to answer "am I making money from marketing?"

**Strategic Role:** ROI Reporting is the "Trojan Horse" -- the entry product for the platform. Easy to sell, fast to deploy, immediate value. Once reporting lives in Ignition, switching costs increase and the upsell path to Intelligence and full platform opens.

---

### Theme 2: AI Insights Agent (Iggy)

**One-line:** Conversational AI agent that proactively surfaces issues, answers natural language questions, and recommends actions from live campaign data.

**Status:** Production (Live)

**Key Benefits:**
- Two modes: Persistent Insights Panel (proactive alerts) and Ask Iggy Chat (conversational Q&A)
- Multi-method health assessment combining goal-based (95% confidence), historical trend (85%), and heuristic/vertical benchmarks (75%)
- Root cause analysis: identifies symptoms, generates hypotheses, gathers evidence, delivers scored recommendations
- Insight lifecycle tracking: New, Acknowledged, In Progress, Resolved, Dismissed -- turning intelligence into accountability
- Iggy Notebook: natural language questions become visual reports with metrics, charts, tables, and insights (saveable, re-runnable, PDF-exportable)

**Target User Persona:** Marketing directors, brand analytics teams, franchise consultants, anyone who needs to ask "what is happening and why?" without waiting for a manual report.

**Strategic Role:** Iggy is the differentiator. No DMP competitor has conversational AI answering questions from live campaign data. Iggy is the reason customers choose Ignition and the reason they stay.

---

### Theme 3: Dynamic AI Creative

**One-line:** AI-generated, brand-compliant creative assets for campaigns, localized automatically for each location.

**Status:** In Development (Target: Q2-Q4 2026)

**Key Benefits:**
- Content generated within brand guidelines -- tone, style, disclaimers, forbidden claims enforced as hard constraints
- Localized variations automatically (address, phone, local offers, regional messaging)
- Multi-format output: social posts, email copy, ad headlines, direct mail text, landing page content
- Compliance audit logging: tracks who generated, downloaded, approved, or rejected every asset

**Target User Persona:** Local operators who need marketing creative but lack design skills or agency budgets; corporate marketing teams who need brand-compliant output at scale.

**Strategic Role:** Three different customers in three different verticals (Janney, Church's Chicken, TJC) independently requested this capability. AI creative is the "wow factor" in demos and the revenue accelerator when combined with ROI Reporting.

---

### Theme 4: Customer Data Platform (CDP)

**One-line:** Unified customer data from CRM, POS, web analytics, and ad platforms -- turning fragmented data into a precision targeting asset.

**Status:** Planned (Target: Q2-Q3 2026)

**Key Benefits:**
- Unified data ingestion and normalization from ad platforms, GA4, CRM, POS, and CSV uploads into shared schemas
- Identity resolution and hierarchy mapping (Brand, Company, Location)
- Predictive segmentation: behavioral segments (purchase patterns), predictive segments (churn risk, upsell readiness), geographic segments
- Segment activation: email/SMS (94% match rate), paid media (87%), in-store/direct mail (98%)

**Target User Persona:** Marketing strategists, CRM managers, data-driven brand teams who want to move from reactive reporting to proactive targeting.

**Strategic Role:** The CDP makes data an appreciating asset. More data flowing through Ignition increases switching costs. CDP is the backbone powering ROI Reporting, Automation, AI Insights, and future predictive models.

---

### Theme 5: Omni-Channel Campaign Management

**One-line:** Print and digital campaigns orchestrated from one platform with unified attribution.

**Status:** Planned (Target: Q3-Q4 2026)

**Key Benefits:**
- Campaign kits and libraries: pre-configured packages with channel mix, creative templates, targeting, budgets, and schedules
- Unified campaign inventory across Google, Meta, LinkedIn, direct mail with status, spend, leads, and ROI in one view
- Direct mail and print integration connected to Ironmark's production systems
- Cross-channel attribution: conversions and revenue attributed consistently across digital and physical touchpoints

**Target User Persona:** Campaign managers, regional marketing leaders, franchise operators deploying localized campaigns.

**Strategic Role:** This is where Ironmark's print heritage becomes a platform advantage. No pure-digital DMP can offer unified print + digital campaign management. Ironmark already produces the physical materials -- Ignition orchestrates them alongside digital.

---

### Theme 6: Distributed Marketing Hub and Brand Asset Management

**One-line:** The "front door" for local operators -- curated programs, co-op fund management, brand-safe templates, and AI recommendations.

**Status:** Planned (Target: Q1-Q2 2027)

**Key Benefits:**
- Program and package catalog: pre-configured campaigns filterable by objective, budget, channel, and expected outcomes
- AI-powered recommendation engine: surfaces programs based on performance gaps, benchmarks, and co-op eligibility
- Co-op and fund management: eligibility checks, claims workflow, proof-of-performance, AI-assisted compliance review
- Brand asset repository with template locks (logo, color, typography, disclaimers) and localizable fields
- Mobile-first design for field-level franchise operators

**Target User Persona:** Franchisees, local operators, branch managers -- people who are operators first and marketers never.

**Strategic Role:** Directly addresses the "local chaos" problem. Converts passive affiliates into active marketers by making marketing as simple as selecting from a menu and pressing go.

---

### Theme 7: Full-Funnel Marketing and Automation

**One-line:** Trigger-based workflows, lead nurturing, and conversion optimization embedded into daily marketing operations.

**Status:** Planned (Target: Q1-Q2 2027)

**Key Benefits:**
- Trigger framework listening to CDP signals (new leads without follow-up, low conversion rates, lapsed customer inactivity)
- Pre-defined drip and nurture playbooks for new leads, abandoned bookings, win-back campaigns
- Visual journey builder: drag-and-drop workflow designer with trigger, action, delay, and condition nodes
- Conversion catalysts: AI lead capture chatbot, call tracking, appointment schedulers
- Lightweight CRM for customers without a full CRM system

**Target User Persona:** Marketing operations teams, franchise corporate teams managing network-wide automation, account managers upselling add-on modules.

**Strategic Role:** Full-funnel automation is the final lock-in. Once Ignition manages the complete workflow from audience identification through conversion tracking, it becomes the operating system for the brand's marketing function.

## 3. Competitive Positioning

### Positioning Frame

**Competitors run marketing. Ignition proves it works.**

The consistent differentiator across every competitor matchup: Ignition answers "is my marketing actually driving revenue at the location level?" when others cannot.

### Top 5 Competitors

#### 1. Ansira (includes BrandMuscle + SproutLoud)

**What they do:** Largest enterprise distributed marketing platform following July 2024 merger. Claims 300+ brands, 1.6M active users. Manages $7.2B in co-op/MDF funds. Named Strong Performer in Forrester Wave: Partner Marketing Automation Platforms, Q2 2025.

**Their strength:** Co-op/MDF fund management (best-in-class), creative compliance and DAM, enterprise-scale operations, broad ecosystem integrations.

**Where Ignition wins:**
- No conversational AI agent -- cannot ask "which locations are underperforming and what should I do about it?" and get a data-driven answer
- G2 reviews cite complexity and steep learning curves; Ignition built for operators, not enterprise admins
- Stops at spend-to-lead; Ignition traces through to revenue at location level
- No native print + digital attribution in a single system

**Positioning:** "Ansira manages distribution and co-op well. Ignition adds intelligence. Can Ansira tell you which of your 500 locations are profitable and why?"

#### 2. SOCi

**What they do:** Local marketing execution at scale. 1,000+ brands, 3M+ locations, 200K+ Genius Agents deployed. AI autonomously publishes posts, responds to reviews, manages listings, executes local campaigns.

**Their strength:** Agentic execution at scale (posts, reviews, listings), 140+ integrations, ISO 42001 certified, mature multi-location operations.

**Where Ignition wins:**
- SOCi shows tasks completed and engagement metrics; Ignition shows revenue attribution
- No conversational analytics layer for marketers (SOCi Chat is consumer-facing)
- Digital-only -- no print or direct mail visibility
- Execution-focused vs. intelligence-focused: SOCi does the work, Ignition measures whether it pays off

**Positioning:** "SOCi executes marketing. Ignition measures if it is working and why. They are execution; we are intelligence."

#### 3. Evocalize

**What they do:** Collaborative marketing platform for multi-location brands. Pre-configured "Blueprints" for rapid local digital campaign deployment. Partnerships with Mastercard, Kumon, UberEats.

**Their strength:** Local operator simplicity, Facebook/Google/TikTok automation, data-triggered campaigns, franchise-scale deployment.

**Where Ignition wins:**
- Spend-to-clicks attribution only; Ignition traces to revenue
- Digital-only -- no print, direct mail, or offline media
- No conversational AI, no root cause analysis, no proactive alerts
- No proprietary data assets

**Positioning:** "Evocalize tells you Location 12 spent $5,000 and got 200 clicks. Ignition tells you Location 12 generated $47,000 in revenue while Location 43 generated $12,000 -- and here is why."

#### 4. Pica9

**What they do:** Brand management and distributed marketing platform focused on franchise organizations. Template customization with guardrails. Most transparent per-location pricing in market ($2-$50/location/month).

**Their strength:** Brand asset management, template compliance, affordability, accessible to smaller franchise systems.

**Where Ignition wins:**
- Template tool, not analytics or intelligence platform
- No revenue attribution, no AI capabilities, no campaign intelligence
- Ignition is reporting + attribution + AI + creative generation; Pica9 is template management

**Positioning:** "Pica9 answers 'are my locations using the right assets?' Ignition answers 'are those assets working?'"

#### 5. Generic Agency Reporting (Most Common Competitive Situation)

**What they do:** Monthly PDF reports from a marketing agency showing impressions, clicks, and spend summaries. This is the status quo for most multilocation brands.

**Their weakness:** Monthly cadence (already outdated), spend/click metrics only (no revenue), brand-level aggregates (no location visibility), no interactivity, no intelligence, agency owns the data.

**Where Ignition wins:**
- Real-time vs. monthly; revenue vs. clicks; location-level vs. aggregates
- Data ownership stays with the brand
- AI agent that prescribes action, not a static PDF

**Positioning:** "Your agency sends you a monthly report that says you spent $200,000 and got 1.2 million impressions. Can they tell you which of your 300 locations are profitable?"

### Key Moats (Non-Financial)

1. **Proprietary Data** -- 160M automotive records via AMMS, growing campaign intelligence across verticals. No competitor owns this data. It fuels AI models and targeting that cannot be replicated.

2. **Agentic AI Architecture** -- Production system with tool chaining, self-correction, and 3-tier model routing (67% cost reduction). Competitors have templates and rules; Ignition has agents that reason, act, and learn.

3. **Intelligence Layer** -- Not just data, but observations from live campaigns: what works vs. what does not, across locations and verticals. Pattern recognition that improves with every customer onboarded.

4. **Full Attribution Pipeline** -- Closed-loop from spend to outcomes to revenue, integrating upstream (ad platforms) and downstream (CRM, POS). Most competitors stop at clicks or leads.

5. **Existing Customer Relationships** -- TJC (900+ locations), Domino's, and others already trust Ironmark. Adding intelligence to relationships that already generate revenue, not selling cold.

## 4. Vertical Summaries

### Healthcare / Chiropractic

**Market Context:** 40,000 enterprise-grade healthcare locations (hospitals, ASCs, urgent care, multi-location physician groups); 70,000 chiropractic clinics. Growing at +2.8% (healthcare) and +0.7% (chiro). Franchise chiro segment (TJC, HealthSource, etc.) growing at 28.5% CAGR.

**Key Pain Points:**
- Clinic managers are not marketers -- wide variance in local marketing skills and execution
- Fragmented tooling (print portals, creative DAMs, digital vendors) with no hierarchical visibility
- Attribution gaps across the funnel: media to call to schedule to visit to revenue
- HIPAA/PHI compliance slows speed to market

**How Ignition Addresses Them:**
- Location-level ROI Reporting: turns 900 scattered reports into one source of truth showing new patient cost and channel performance by clinic
- Iggy AI for clinic benchmarking: surfaces insights in plain language ("Your new patient cost is 34% above network average; here is what top clinics do differently")
- Patient acquisition attribution: connects campaign spend to call tracking, appointment booking, and new patient visits
- HIPAA-aware workflows and privacy-first architecture

**Proof Point:** The Joint Chiropractic -- 900+ locations live on Ignition (February 2026).

---

### QSR / Restaurant

**Market Context:** 204,000 QSR franchise locations. Largest addressable segment. 82% operated by multi-unit franchisees. +2.2% YoY growth.

**Key Pain Points:**
- LTO execution is fragmented and inconsistent at store level -- corporate does not know if stores are executing campaigns
- Disconnected data (POS, loyalty, app/web, delivery marketplaces)
- Heavy lift to localize creative across hundreds of stores for each LTO window
- Co-op/ad fund dollars deployed on faith with no ROI proof

**How Ignition Addresses Them:**
- Campaign performance by location: store-by-store view for every active LTO campaign, not just brand-level averages
- Channel attribution for local marketing: connects spend to same-store traffic indicators and average ticket movement
- Network benchmarking: surfaces top-quartile franchisee behavior and packages it as actionable recommendations for underperformers

**Proof Point:** Domino's targeted for March 2026 onboarding. QSR-specific campaign kits (LTO Booster, New-Store Launch) designed for the platform.

---

### Automotive

**Market Context:** 18,000 franchised dealership rooftops. Average revenue per dealership ~$70.8M. Top 150 dealer groups control 33% of industry revenue. Essentially flat growth.

**Key Pain Points:**
- Each rooftop running its own marketing through its own vendors -- no aggregate visibility
- OEM co-op programs are a compliance nightmare; money left on the table annually
- Service marketing (fixed ops) under-leveraged vs. sales despite higher margins
- Siloed systems (DMS, CRM, inventory feeds, websites, call tracking)

**How Ignition Addresses Them:**
- AMMS data asset: 160M automotive consumer records enabling audience segments no competitor can build (in-market buyers, service mileage intervals)
- Unified sales + service ROI reporting: campaign spend mapped to VDP views, leads, appointments, and closed repair orders by rooftop
- OEM co-op compliance workflow: pre-approved templates, auto-generated offer footnotes, documentation meeting OEM submission requirements

**Proof Point:** AMMS is in production generating measurable value. Channel partners (DealerWing, Dynatron) already white-labeling Ironmark solutions in automotive.

---

### Financial Services

**Market Context:** 98,000 locations (76,000 bank branches + 22,000 credit union offices). -1.5% YoY (branch consolidation trend, though JPMorgan expanding). Excludes ~135K-145K insurance agencies (TAM upside). 9-18 month sales cycles.

**Key Pain Points:**
- Strict compliance (FINRA/SEC/GLBA) slows campaigns; fragmented review loops
- Branches and advisors either go rogue or do not market at all -- no middle ground
- Hard to scale local advisor marketing while maintaining brand safety and audit readiness
- Cannot connect marketing spend to qualified appointments, proposals, or funded accounts

**How Ignition Addresses Them:**
- Pre-approved creative and compliance workflow: moves compliance upstream so advisors deploy from approved libraries without individual reviews
- Branch-level benchmarking: surfaces marketing activity rates, campaign participation, and pipeline attribution by branch, region, and advisor
- Full audit trail: timestamps, approval records, disclosure versions for every piece of advisor-generated marketing

**Proof Point:** Compliance workflow, pre-approved template libraries, and RBAC are built into the Ignition MVP. Preferra and Janney in pipeline.

---

### Franchise / Multi-Unit (General)

**Market Context:** 647,000 non-QSR franchise locations across personal services, retail, home services, and more. +2.5% YoY growth. The broadest segment in the TAM.

**Key Pain Points:**
- Franchisees either do nothing with their local marketing budget or go off-brand
- Ad fund contributions collected but ROI on fund deployment difficult to demonstrate
- Best franchisees figured out what works; worst do not know what to run; no scalable way to close the gap

**How Ignition Addresses Them:**
- One-click campaign kits: everything a franchisee needs to run a brand-approved campaign in a single deployable unit
- Location benchmarking with Iggy recommendations in plain language ("You are in the bottom quartile -- top performers run two additional campaigns; here is how to match them")
- Ad fund and co-op visibility: connects fund spend to network-level and location-level outcomes

**Proof Point:** TJC validates the franchise model at 900+ locations. Home Instead, South Moon Under, Madden Plumbing, and others in MVP cohort.

## 5. Tone and Voice Recommendations

### Audience Profile

The internal KB audience is non-technical Ironmark employees: salespeople, account managers, marketing staff, and executives. They need to:
- Understand what Ignition does and why it matters
- Explain it to customers in their own words
- Know what is live today vs. what is on the roadmap
- Handle competitive questions confidently

### Recommended Voice

**Confident, direct, and conversational.** Not overly technical. Not corporate-speak. Write like an experienced colleague explaining the product to a new team member over coffee.

**Specific guidance:**

1. **Lead with the problem, not the product.** Every page should start with the pain the customer feels before describing how Ignition solves it. Mirror the buyer's voice -- use quotes and scenarios they would recognize.

2. **Use "you" and "we" language.** "We" is Ironmark. "You" is the internal reader. Avoid third-person distancing ("the platform enables stakeholders to..."). Instead: "Ignition shows you which locations are winning and why."

3. **No jargon without explanation.** Terms like "through-channel," "CDP," "closed-loop attribution," and "multi-tenant" should be used but always accompanied by a plain-English explanation the first time they appear. Consider a glossary page.

4. **Concrete over abstract.** Instead of "AI-powered insights," say "Iggy tells a clinic manager their new patient cost is 34% above network average and recommends what the top clinics do differently." Use the Location 12 vs. Location 43 pattern to make abstract benefits concrete.

5. **Honest about status.** Clearly distinguish what is live in production today (ROI Reporting, Iggy) from what is in development (Dynamic AI Creative) and what is planned (CDP, Omni-Channel, Hub, Automation). Internal audiences need accurate information to set customer expectations correctly.

6. **Vertical-native language.** Each vertical section should use that vertical's terminology. Healthcare talks about patients and clinics, not leads and stores. QSR talks about LTOs and guest counts. Automotive talks about rooftops and repair orders. The terminology guide from the GTM enablement docs should be embedded in each vertical section.

7. **Short paragraphs, scannable structure.** Internal readers are looking for answers, not reading cover-to-cover. Use headers, tables, bullet points, and bold text for key terms. Each page should be usable in 90 seconds.

8. **No financial details.** Exclude pricing, revenue projections, ARR targets, deal sizes, and valuation multiples. This is product marketing, not a financial pitch.
